About This Site

In 2021–2024, UCLA convened the Research Data Working Group (RDWG) — a campus-wide effort commissioned by five executive sponsors (VCR, University Librarian, AVC & CIO, Vice Provost for IT, and CIO of Health Sciences) to assess UCLA’s research data infrastructure and recommend a path forward. The 18-month effort included campus engagement, peer benchmarking across 13 service categories at comparable institutions, and a detailed implementation analysis.

This site presents the RDWG’s key findings, recommendations, and supporting evidence for the One IT Research Working Group (charged by the EVCP and CIO, March 2026).

Key Findings in Brief

  • UCLA falls behind peers in 8 of 13 research data service categories, including the most basic: a cohesive program with a service catalog.
  • 90% of UCLA’s $7M annual enterprise storage spend occurs at the departmental level — fragmented, redundant, and insecure.
  • There is no central ownership of research data services. PIs navigate compliance requirements, storage crises, and funder mandates alone.
  • UCLA’s NSF per-award average ($307K) is 15–30% below UCSB and UCSD. Missing infrastructure for large multi-site proposals is a contributing factor.
  • Funder mandates have outpaced UCLA’s capacity. The NIH Data Management and Sharing Policy (January 2023) and OSTP public access mandate (December 2025) require institutional infrastructure UCLA does not yet have.

RDWG Priority Proposals

The RDWG recommended a layered model — not consolidation, but coordination. Central services raise the floor; local expertise is preserved where research happens.

ORDR and the Bruin Data Hub are the foundational layer — coordination and storage that make the rest possible. Services and infrastructure build on both.

UCLA already has a production-ready sensitive data platform. UCLA Library operates UCLA Redivis — a multi-organization sensitive data repository with SSO, Data Use Agreement management, DOI minting, and Python/R APIs. It is already institutionalized and adopted by peer research universities. Any WG recommendation on P3/P4 sensitive data should build on Redivis rather than duplicate it.
